"Legends speak of a towering shade,
In black he walks, through twilight laid, Red thread wound tight on his crooked back
A burden spun from blood and black. When snowflakes drift through silent trees,
And laughter echoes on the breeze, Mothers cry for their young to come,
But a bell tolls low too late, they're gone.
With steps that make no sound at all, He glides beneath the snow's white pall.
Where children stray, where candles fade, He gathers souls the woods have claimed.
A beast's skull crowns his hooded head, Its antlers hung with charms of dread
Bells that jingle, softly cursed, To warn the woods: things may get worse.
They say he weaves the world's regret, That magic came with bloodied debt.
Each spell you cast, each charm you dare, Pulls from the spool of Leythafer.
So mark this rhyme, and do not stray, When snow begins and skies turn gray.
For threads of red still stitch the air, And none return who meet Leythafer. 

A young voice echoed softly, reciting the last line in rhythm. She giggled and flopped backward, caught by a sofa made of bones and fur.
"I don't like that song," she said, scrunching
her nose. "It's full of lies and scary stories."
The being turned its head toward her, slow and soundless. Small bells jingled gently with the motion.

"...But," she added, twirling a bit of thread between her fingers, "you did find me on a moonless night, in the snow.
 So... I like it anyway. It reminds me of when we met."
The beast's expression did not change its skull-face forever fixed in silence but something in the air shifted, as if it were thinking.
 Then, softly, began to hum. The sound was low, like wind through frostbitten trees.
"When the sky grew dim and the ground
turned pale,
A bell rang out, clear as day.
The spell-weaver came through drifting snow
It sensed a fever down below.
A skunk lay shivering, half-entombed, Its breath was short, its scent consumed.
So a spell I spun, not one of harm
But one of love: a coat, kept warm.
A little one, by the world cast aside... We're the same, you and I.
A charm I wove, once... then twice,
And whispered my name to still your fright. To ward off sorrow. To bind the air.
I am Leythafer."
Silence filled the cave...
"I think I like this one more..." the child hid its face.
The Leythafer continued weaving, the fireplace crackled as tiny sticks slowly broke and became ash, a spectacle only a child would give this much attention to.
