Before the elves, before the people, long before the rise of society, there was only chaos.

A young man, shivering against the merciless storm, wandered the accursed earth, driven only by the desperate need to survive another endless night. Hunger sang to him like the sweetest of fairies, whispering temptations in his ear. Seeking refuge, he stumbled upon a cave, its depths offering shelter from the relentless winds. But he was not alone.

Inside lay a dragon, its mighty wings battered by the storm, unable to take flight.

"May I stay here and share your shelter from the storm?" the young man asked, though hunger gnawed at his resolve.

The dragon regarded him with weary eyes. "As I am now, it is not my choice what you do with me. Look outside, see how the storm rages. In this world, only the strong decide."

The man smiled faintly. "Then... I will sit."

The dragon blinked in surprise.

"I am Faelan, dragon of wind and storm."

"Wind and storm?" the man echoed. "And yet you're grounded?Perhaps the world truly is ending." He chuckled, the irony not lost on him.

Faelan let out a rumbling laugh of his own before his voice grew somber. "Perhaps it is. I can no longer command the winds… but more than that, I fear the world is unraveling, for here I am, speaking with a man. With every word, the old rules fracture."

The young man’s gaze softened. "Isn’t that a good thing?" he mused. "I don’t like a world where I can’t make friends. If I were king, I’d build a place where everyone was equal… where we’d help the wounded and—"

"Guide the lost," the dragon finished.



The flightless dragon and the man journeyed toward Erev’s peak, their figures casting long shadows against the fading light. As they walked, the man, with his warmth and charm, drew the attention of the fairies and spirits of Erev, their whispers dancing in the air like a song carried by the wind.

As they neared the peak.

“Is it enough?” the man asked, troubled. “For a man and a dragon to heal this world?”

Faelan exhaled, the wind stirring at his breath. “I do not know. But if the answer were simple, someone would have done it already.” A pause, then a knowing glance. “And I know you’d hate that.”

The man laughed, his voice carrying through the mountain air.

And so, they climbed on.

“Is strength justice?” the young man asked, his voice carried by the wind.

“It is in this world,” the dragon breathed.

“Then weakness would be—”

“A crime,” they said together.

For a moment, silence stretched between them, heavy with understanding.

“We should start with that,” the man spoke at last.

The dragon’s dart green eyes flickered in the dim light. “It appears we already have.”

And so, they continued their journey, step by step, word by word, shaping a new truth with each breath.

On a dark night, much like the one they first met, they lay beside a crackling fire, embers rising like tiny stars into the void. The dragon, once mighty, rested his weary body against the stone, while the man gazed into the flames, lost in thought.

“Once we reach the peak, you’ll be able to command the winds again…” The man turned to Faelan, his expression unreadable. “And then?”

“We’ll use the winds to send a message,” the dragon said, his voice steady with purpose. “To all who have suffered, to all who still hope.”

The man let out a low chuckle, the embers of the fire flickering in his golden eyes. “Then may I speak into the winds?”

The dragon nodded

At last, they reached the peak, the winds whispering their arrival.

“Our journey has ended,” the man said, his voice heavy with the weight of the climb.

Faelan, standing tall against the sky, shook his mighty head. “Perhaps it has only just begun, I’ll keep the shadows at bay, you gather our kin, dear friend.”

He paused, the winds stirring as he spoke again.

“Aelorion... the Wind’s Blade. By my decree, you must gather them, the lost and the wandering, and guide them here,” Faelan’s voice was deep, like a storm gathering on the horizon. “Build your kingdom, oh first king of the elves.”
