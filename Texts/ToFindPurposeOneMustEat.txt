There was no child of mine without purpose.

…Except Isilacirca.

Erythremir was a bloodwell.
Stagnate was a breeding pool.
Kynnmorrow was a hunter.

And since I already had a hunter, why would I need another?
Isilacirca was made without purpose hollow and empty, precisely as I required. But do not be deceived. Its purpose was sacred. So sacred that I alone named it.
Isilacirca//from Isila, and the ancient word circa.

Names hold power.

To be born hollow and yet showered with love is a curse of exquisite design:
wanting to feel, yet unable;
wanting to see, yet having no eyes;
needing to feast, yet having no food.

Isilacirca was always empty just as Erythremir was meant to be, before desire betrayed him.
Of all my children, Isilacirca craved purpose the most. It longed for a voice to scream with, a mind to think with, a self to be. And so, it came to pass.
I planted a seed.

And in its search for purpose, Isilacirca grew vast, grotesque, magnificent. It sought dragons: the only beasts that ever dared look down upon me.

Those flying, scaled hypocrites.
They believe themselves mighty brave, clever, righteous, or vile by choice.
They posture as judges of right and wrong.

They do not know what righteousness is.
But to Isilacirca, that did not matter.
Through its ocelli, dragons appeared to possess personality. And so it believed that if it devoured enough of them, it too would grow one.

I hated dragons.
Being hunted by Kynnmorrow was not punishment enough for their existence. Isilacirca was their sentence.

Not to kill//but to cultivate fear.
Not to hunt//but to grant hope of escape…

…only to tear it away.

Isilacirca.

But after my departure from this plane having never grown a personality, having never sated this hunger, Isilacirca will keep on feasting. Truly a terrifying design
