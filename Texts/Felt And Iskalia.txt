Felt, Secret of the Elves:

Felt, first princess of the Elven Kingdom and fourth in line to the throne, was born on a night darker than most. Though elves claimed to be above rumors, whispers spread quickly Felt was a cursed child. Her father, King Aurelion, outlawed such talk, yet the rumors were not entirely baseless.

From birth, Felt was fragile and weak, her body marked with an enigmatic symbol no scholar could explain. Her parents doted on her, tending to her every need. They played with her, listened to her stories of an unseen friend, and shielded her from the world. But they were rulers, and even loving parents could not watch forever.

One fateful day, the adventurous young princess climbed a tree, only to fall onto a jagged rock. When the maids and knights reached her, they found her lifeless body, and the king was called in haste. Yet when Aurelion arrived, he saw not a dead child, but a terrified one her strange mark pulsing with dark energy. The rock, the supposed cause of her death, was gone as if it had never been there.

From that day on, Felt was confined within the castle walls. The story of a cursed princess was rewritten now, she was simply the sickly child of King Aurelion and Queen Artel. To her father, she was something to protect. To her mother, just a child. To her brothers, a fragile burden. But to the kingdom, she was a legend of fear.

Alone in a hidden garden known only to the royal family, Felt spent her days speaking with the one voice in her mind she deemed “even lonelier than I.” She read, danced, and trained, dreaming of the day she might find a cure, reclaim her place, and prove she was more than a ghost of a forgotten tale.

Iskalia, the First of Witches.

Wherever she roamed, she wove a veil of darkness, and fear and shadow followed in her every step. The world beneath her touch was hushed, shrouded in night, and in those forgotten days if they could be called days Iskalia was happy. She was never alone, for she walked beside her dearest of friends, creatures born from the abyss, unseen by the eyes of mortals.

But then came the light.

It seared the earth, flooding the skies, blinding Iskalia and scorching her beloved kin. They writhed and wailed, their forms dissolving under its merciless radiance. Terrified, wounded, and seething with fury, Iskalia ascended Mount Erev, her voice echoing through the heavens as she struck at the glass sky, seeking to shatter it, to drown the world in shadow once more.

But the sky did not break.

With no other choice, she gathered her dying friends and pulled them into herself, enfolding them in her own shadow. Their darkness seeped into her skin, turning it pitch black, a living abyss. And so, the First Witch vanished from the world, becoming both prison and sanctuary for those she loved.

But the world had not forgotten her.

And neither had she forgotten the world.



Having mended her wounds from the light, Iskalia waited. And on a dark September night, she found a vessel.

A child. A princess of the elves. Felt.

This mortal body would be her means of revenge. Her tool to shroud the world in shadow once more. But the child… the child was different.

Felt was kind. She was gentle. She did not fight against Iskalia’s presence, nor did she fear her. Instead, she spoke to her, laughed with her. And soon, she called her “my only friend.”

Iskalia grew weak. How could she destroy this child, when in Felt’s eyes, she saw herself? Every day, she whispered apologies for burdening her.

Years passed, and as Felt grew, Iskalia faded. She became a mere whisper, hiding in the depths of the girl's soul. Felt barely remembered the voice from her childhood//only a distant echo of comfort and warmth. She moved forward, while Iskalia, left behind, withered in the silence.

And in that silence, something broke.

The abyssal flames of vengeance had never been quenched. Without Felt’s voice, without her warmth, the shadows curdled into madness. Iskalia twisted, hungrier, crueler, more violent.

Felt had moved on. But Iskalia had not.
