	Spirits are born with purpose—we are beings of meaning, our fate decided with our very first breath. And like all spirits, I, too, was born with a purpose I found noble: to guard, to protect.

So I did just that. I stood behind my queen, unmoving, unspeaking. Never once did I object. Never once did I complain.

And so came another night.

The sky, an endless abyss, wept streams of sorrow as thousands of spirits passed through the gates wounded, seeking the sanctuary of our queen. The heavens had been swallowed whole, the light stripped away, leaving behind only a void.

I was there for it all.

The Abyss the sky had become was poison to us spirits, a chasm where light had simply vanished, leaving behind nothing, simply nothing. I listened to my queen’s words, to her worries, always standing by, always present. It was as if she spoke for me, and so I was content to simply be there.

When young spirits took their final breaths, their forms fading into the ether, something stirred in my chest, a feeling that I should help, that I should speak and ease their passing. But my queen always went before me, as a queen should, not a lowly guard. There were nights i felt as if i wanted to shout, and scream, to weep and curl up in sorrow, but seeing my queen being as sad or even sadder I stood strong, yet again as a statue I stood there.

Then, on a day like all the rest lightless and stagnant, he came. A man followed by a dragon. A man who smiled in the face of darkness. He spoke to the beast as though it were his friend, as though the world itself had never known despair. He spread his arms as if to embrace all creation, and with that reckless confidence, he wove promises of rekindled skies, of dreams and distant horizons, how can one dream in the nights of no end? I didn’t say that outloud of course, as always with bow in hand i guarded my queen as he conversed with her, but as he spoke of dreams and hopes with her, his eyes were cast on me. What an insollent man, a simple mortal speaks to the queen of spirits, and he speaks of such nonsense, while doing that he never once looks her in the eyes, he chooses to watch me... a guard, how insolent. My eye slightly twitched as I thought of the man, he made me seethe with anger, he didn’t know what I ...or my queen have been through in these endless nights...


     But then he spoke, cutting of her majesty, he opened his mouth and let out a hearty laughter “So you really aren’t a doll, a statue nor a painting”, his gaze directed on me...I was taken aback, not visible, but for some reason i could sense he knew I did. For a moment, silence reigned. Even the rain seemed to hush, the air thick with something unspoken. My fingers twitched against the bowstring, though I had no arrow nocked. I should have ignored him. Should have continued to stand still, to let my queen speak for me as she always had. And yet… something about his words, about his eyes that bore into mine with unshaken certainty, made my lips part ever so slightly. I did not speak, but it was enough for him to notice.

My queen, charmed by this man, as all the spirits seemed to be gave shelter to him and his companion.

My next meeting with him was during my nightly patrols. The halls were as silent as the sky was dark, the air thick with the weight of eternity. The spirits who had sought refuge in our queen’s domain lay in uneasy rest, their forms flickering like dying embers. I moved as I always had soundless, watchful, unseen.

And yet, he noticed me.

Aelorion had been given a place of luxury, chambers fit for a guest of high standing. Yet, rather than take his rest, he had ventured from his room, drawn instead to the vast windows overlooking the royal gardens. His friend lay below, coiled in peaceful slumber, its great form rising and falling with each breath.

He sat upon the window’s edge, one knee drawn up, the other leg dangling over the side as if he were perched upon a cliff. His back was to me, his posture utterly at ease. And yet, before I could even announce my presence, he spoke.

“Tell me, guardian. Do you dream?” Aelorion mused, leaning forward. Instinctively, I shifted my stance, my grip tightening on my bow. He did not falter, nor did his dragon.

My fingers curled against my palm. I should have walked away. Should have continued my patrol, left him to his foolish musings. But something about him unsettled me. Something about the way he spoke, the way he carried himself as though he belonged here// as though he belonged anywhere //made my feet stay rooted to the floor.

“A guest of the queen should not wander unattended”, I spoke...soon realising how many years had passed since the last time i made sound.

“Am I unattended? You are here, after all”, he answered before then adding “Or do you not count as a person?”. The question struck deeper than I cared to admit, he seems to have noticed even that, he had many flaws in my eyes but one thing he wasnt was rude. The point was that he was different. That he did not carry the sorrow we all bore, that he was not bound to this eternal night like the rest of us. The point was that he made me feel seen in a way I had never been before. He turned fully then, swinging his leg back over the ledge and standing in one smooth motion. The way he moved it was fluid, effortless, like he was untouched by the weariness that clung to the rest of us. He faced me fully, and for the first time, there was no teasing in his expression.

“Apologies, really...”. An exhale was heard from the gardens, as if the dragon was awake and wanted to laugh, the man soon turned his back to me and headed towards his chambers...There really was no need for me to do so, but i gathered some air in my lungs and quietly said “Goodnight”, perhaps i had missed talking, or I really wished to converse with him a bit more.

The following nights were as always the same abyss, the same darkness that flooded the skies. My patrols remained unchanged, the halls still draped in silence, yet somehow, I was no longer alone.

He was always there. And yet, despite my better judgment, I found myself seeking him out. Not deliberately, not consciously, but my steps always seemed to lead me near. And when they did, he spoke “You never answered me,” his golden eyes shimering bellow the moonless sky. His dragon lay curled at the foot of the fountain, as if waiting for something.

I knew what he meant “I do not dream”, I simply answered. Aelorion turned studying me “Because you cannot? Or because you do not allow yourself to?”

Inside me I frowned before speaking yet again to this man “It is not a matter of allowance. It is a matter of nature.” The dragon opened one of his eyes with a knowing smirk, Aelorion with a thoughtful hum and a slow breath mused “Yet you said you do not, instead of can not”, The distinction unsettled me.

“Then what do you do, spirit?” the beast asked. Its voice was deep, ancient, tinged with a knowing that set my form rigid. “What is it to exist without wonder?” I found myself silent once more, but this time i really had no answer, perhaps because i never needed one

Aelorion did not press, but his gaze lingered, heavy with thought. Then, with a quiet chuckle, he turned to his companion. “You’ll scare her off if you keep that up Faelan.”

“Then do you think it is a matter of time?” he turned to the dragon, and the beast nodded, i hated how synchronised they were, but truly Time. A meaningless thing in a realm where night never ended.

For days, my thoughts were no longer my own. My mind, once an empty vessel of duty, now teemed with restless questions//questions I had no right to ask. That arrogant man, that foolish dreamer, had interrupted my existence without even lifting a finger. How aggravating.

That night, I was different. I was no longer the silent sentinel, no longer the unseen shadow. Under the guise of my patrols, I stormed toward the place where we met each night, my steps heavy with something I did not yet understand. Tears—foreign, unwelcome—slid down my cheekbones, hot against the cold stillness of my form.

Aelorion was already there, waiting. As if he had known I would come//infuriating//. The dragon lay nearby, watching with unreadable eyes. He regarded me, but before he could speak, I shattered the silence.

“Yes, I do not know how to live,” I choked out, my voice raw, unpracticed. “You win.” The words burned like confession, like surrender. “But it is not as if I chose this. I am a spirit. Our fates are decided at birth.”

Aelorion’s expression softened, his usual mirth replaced with something quieter concern, perhaps? He stepped closer, his movements unhurried, deliberate. Then, without hesitation, he knelt before me, lowering himself to my level. He studied my face for a long moment.

“We write our own fates. Don’t you see? You chose to speak. You chose to come here.” His gaze was steady, unwavering. “Your feelings… they are proof that you are alive.”

I wanted to deny him. To tell him he was wrong. But I couldn’t. I was trembling, my breath uneven, my very being unraveling at the seams.

His laughter faded, and in its place, a whisper.

“I promise you I will make a world where you do not have to cry because you feel. A world where you will your own fate.”

I lifted my head from where I had been staring at the ground, my vision blurred with tears. I had never once raised my voice. I had never once spoken from the depths of my being. Until now.

“Show me,” I pleaded, the words ripping free from my chest. “Show me how to live. Show me how to laugh, Aelorion please…please show me the sky.”

For a moment, he only looked at me. The man grinned as if destiny called his name, he got up and extended his arm to me.

“Come with me then”...
