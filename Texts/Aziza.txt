"A purge is needed. These godless freaks are taking up our space, filthying our streets. Who here hates it when they're on a walk and one of them grabs you by the foot, begging and licking for change?!" The unrest of the crowd grew even larger as hands shot to the sky in unison.
Aziza, wearing a hood amidst the crowd, scoffed, the fire escaping her breath almost exposing her to the angry mob.
"It is the church's work to rid the streets of such devils*the lower, godless class who begs us to melt our statues of worship. How blasphemous they are. If the gods did love them, then food would be no issue. For this I say: away! Yes, away, demons who have forsaken god in the race of riches."
The hypocrisy made Aziza laugh as she made her way through the crowd toward the front. "All they care about is images and money. This could work, Flare," she said, patting the small drake hidden in her cloak with a self-satisfied grin.
"Alas, we shall continue ridding the streets of them, so that the gods may shine bright for us once more..." The speaker took off his hat and began walking, collecting fees and tributes, before he passed over a cloaked, small figure.
Aziza put her metal gauntlet to her mouth and breathed fire over it//her left hand already flaming hot from the drake concealed within her clothes. With a quick snap she closed her hand around the man's wrist.
"AHH — DAMN IT, CATCH HER!"
She was off, running on all fours before finding her balance. She counted the gold in her hands; truly a shame, for she did not care for such riches. Paladins and angry men of the mob followed close behind.
"I'LL SNAP HER NECK — SEE, MEN AND WOMEN OF GOD, HOW THEY HURT US! HOW THEIR FILTH RUNS DEEP, EVEN IN OUR SMALL COMMUNE!"
Hearing this, Aziza knew it was time. A dead end loomed ahead. She threw the gold as high as she could and breathed fire into the alley to stop her chasers dead in their tracks. She knew she could never escape with the riches after pulling off such a public stunt. She turned toward a hidden passage only runts like her knew, but an angry man fired at her with a crossbow, scarring her left shoulder badly. She shrugged it off and made it out.
Sliding her back against the wall, she was now safe, breathing heavily to affirm the fact. Flare, the brownish dragon, flew over her soon after, dropping a piece of paper onto her lap.
A map*one pointing to a dungeon where other street rats like her were kept, and beyond that, many artifacts of the church. She scoffed fire again as she tied cloth around the wound. Heist successful. "Nice one, Flare."
Two years passed and Aziza turned sixteen, her appetite for heists undiminished. She walked the main streets of the slums, catching an apple thrown to her by those she had once saved. The city knew her now, or knew enough to leave her be. But the city was no longer enough. Her latest lead pointed well beyond its walls, and wherever there were dungeons, there were runts like her, and things worth taking.
